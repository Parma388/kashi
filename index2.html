Dev Deepawali

Dev Deepawali, also known as the "Diwali of the Gods," is a spectacular festival celebrated primarily in Varanasi, India, on the full moon night of Kartik Purnima, fifteen days after Diwali. This festival marks the victory of Lord Shiva over the demon Tripurasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. On this night, the ghats of the Ganges are illuminated with millions of earthen lamps (diyas), creating a mesmerizing sight. Devotees gather to perform rituals, chant hymns, and take a holy dip in the river, believing it brings them closer to the divine.

Mhashivratri

Mahashivratri in Varanasi is a deeply spiritual and vibrant celebration dedicated to Lord Shiva. The city, considered Shiva’s eternal abode, s alive with devotees thronging temples like Kashi Vishwanath. Throughout the night, special prayers, hymns, and rituals are performed with great devotion. The Ganga River’s ghats are beautifully illuminated, and the air fills with the chants of "Om Namah Shivaya." Pilgrims fast and meditate, seeking blessings for prosperity and spiritual growth. The festival symbolizes the triumph of light over darkness and ignorance. Experiencing Mahashivratri in Varanasi offers a unique blend of faith, culture, and divine energy.
